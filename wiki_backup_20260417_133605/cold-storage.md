# Cold Storage

**Summary**: The practice of keeping cryptocurrency private keys completely offline to protect them from digital attacks and unauthorized access.

**Sources**: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security-6.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

---

## Overview
Cold storage refers to any method of storing private keys that ensures they are not exposed to an internet-connected device. This is the most effective defense against remote hacking, malware, and unauthorized digital access (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security-6.md).

## Implementation Methods
Common methods of cold storage include:
- **Paper Wallets**: Printing private keys or seed phrases on physical paper.
- **Hardware Wallets**: Using specialized devices that keep keys in a secure element and sign transactions internally without ever exposing the key to the OS (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash-chapter-8-security-7.md).
- **Air-gapped Computers**: Using a computer that has never been and will never be connected to a network.

## Trade-off: Security vs. Accessibility
While cold storage maximizes security, it reduces the speed and ease of spending. Moving funds from cold storage to a "hot" wallet (like a mobile wallet) is required to initiate a transaction (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash-chapter-8-security-8.md).
