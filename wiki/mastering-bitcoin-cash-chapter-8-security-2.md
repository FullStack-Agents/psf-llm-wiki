# Mastering [Bitcoin Cash](bitcoin-cash.md) Chapter 8 Security 2

**Summary**: Comparison between decentralized and centralized security models, highlighting why [Bitcoin Cash](bitcoin-cash.md) does not require network encryption but places a high burden on [private key](private-keys.md) management.

**Sources**: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_2.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

---

Traditional payment networks (e.g., credit cards) use open-ended payments with reusable user identifiers. This architecture requires end-to-end encryption to prevent the compromise of payment tokens and current transactions; breaches often lead to identity theft (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_2.md).

In contrast, [Bitcoin Cash](bitcoin-cash.md) transactions authorize only specific values to specific recipients and do not reveal private information. Because these transactions cannot be reused or modified, they do not require encrypted transmission. It is safe to broadcast [Bitcoin Cash](bitcoin-cash.md) transactions over unsecured channels, such as public WiFi, without compromising security (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_2.md).

However, this decentralized model shifts the security burden to the user, who must securely manage their [private keys](private-keys.md). This task is often challenging when using general-purpose computing devices (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_2.md).
