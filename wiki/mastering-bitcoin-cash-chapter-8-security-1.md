# Mastering [bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) Chapter 8 Security 1

**Summary**: Introduction to the unique nature of [bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) security, emphasizing its role as digital cash and the central role of decentralization.

**Sources**: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_1.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

---

[bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) functions as digital cash rather than an abstract value reference. A key characteristic is that possession of keys equals full ownership, meaning there is no recourse for loss or theft (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_1.md). Unlike physical assets, digital wallets can be backed up in multiple copies, including physical paper backups.

The fundamental security principle of [bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) is [decentralization](decentralization.md). While traditional payment systems rely on access control and network encryption, [bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) shifts responsibility to users through [proof of work](proof-of-work.md). Consequently, [bitcoin-cash](bitcoin-cash.md) transactions do not require network encryption because they only authorize specific values to specific recipients and are designed to be impossible to forge or modify (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_1.md).
