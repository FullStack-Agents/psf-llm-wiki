## The Peril of Over-Securing
While preventing theft is critical, users must balance security with accessibility. Excessive security measures can lead to the permanent loss of funds if the keys or decryption methods are lost. An example of this occurred in July 2011, where a bitcoin education project lost approximately 7,000 bitcoin because the encryption keys for their complex backups were lost (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_8.md).

## Risk Diversification Strategy
Rather than relying on a single security solution, a balanced approach involves distributing holdings across multiple wallet types. This minimizes the impact of a single point of failure.

A recommended distribution strategy includes:
- **Online/Mobile Wallets**: Maintain a small percentage (e.g., 5%) for daily transactions and accessibility.
- **Desktop/Offline Storage**: Split the remaining funds between desktop wallets and offline cold storage solutions (source: mastering-bitcoin-cash_chapter-8-bitcoin-cash-security_8.md).
